Trying to type the Capital Ñ (Big Enye) or the small ñ (lowercase) but your compact laptop or mechanical keyboard doesn't have a dedicated numeric keypad on the right side?

Most standard keyboard tutorials tell you to hold the Alt key and type 164 or 165 on the number pad. However, if you are using a modern thin laptop, notebook, or a TKL mechanical keyboard, those Alt codes simply won't work. Don't worry—you don't need to change your hardware. This master guide will show you the easiest hidden shortcuts to type both the big Enye (Ñ) and small enye (ñ) variants without any physical numeric keypad.

Method 1: Use US-International Keyboard (The Fastest Way)

The absolute best way to type special characters on a compact laptop is by activating the United States-International keyboard layout. This layout converts your standard keys into dynamic accent creators.

How to Activate it on Windows:

  1. Open Windows Settings (Press Win + I).
  2. Go to Time & Language > Click on Language & Region.
  3. Click the three dots next to English (US) and select Language Options.
  4. Under Keyboards, click Add a keyboard and choose United States-International.
  5. Switch to this layout anytime by pressing Windows Key + Spacebar.
To Type Small ñ (Lowercase Enye): Press the Right Alt Key + N simultaneously on your keyboard.
To Type Capital Ñ (Big Enye): Press Right Alt Key + Shift + N at the same time to render the uppercase character instantly.

Method 2: Use Windows Virtual On-Screen Keyboard

If you don't want to change your system's layout profiles, you can easily use the built-in virtual touch keyboard:

  • Right-click your Windows taskbar and select "Show touch keyboard button".
  • Click the digital keyboard icon in your system tray to display the visual keyboard.
  • Click and hold down the standard n key.
  • A tiny sub-menu bubble layer will pop up. Select ñ. To type the big Enye (Ñ), press the Shift key on the touch layout first, then hold down N.

Method 3: Copy via Windows Character Map Utility

Windows provides a library utility called Character Map that holds every single diacritical mark:

  • Press Win + R to open the Run box.
  • Type charmap and hit Enter.
  • Locate the Enye characters.
  • Double-click on the Capital Ñ or small ñ, click 'Copy', and paste it directly into your document.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Q: Why doesn't Alt + 164 work on my laptop keys?

A: Standard Alt codes require numeric signals generated exclusively by a dedicated physical numeric keypad row on the right side of the keyboard. The standard number row above the letters will not trigger it.

Q: Does MS Word have a special shortcut for laptops without numpads?

A: Yes! Inside Word, you can press Ctrl + Shift + ~ simultaneously, release, and then type n for small enye, or N for the big Enye character.
